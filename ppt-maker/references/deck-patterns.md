# Deck Patterns

## Investor Deck

- Decision: take the meeting seriously and continue diligence.
- Sequence: problem, insight, solution, product, market, traction, GTM, moat, team, ask.
- Bias toward proof, speed, and momentum.
- Must include: a strong opening claim, evidence pages, and a concrete ask close.

## Sales Deck

- Decision: move the buyer to the next commercial step.
- Sequence: pain, cost of inaction, solution, proof, workflow, ROI, rollout, CTA.
- Bias toward buyer language and quantified outcomes.
- Must include: proof, workflow clarity, and rollout confidence.

## Project Update

- Decision: align on status, risks, and next actions.
- Sequence: headline, goals, progress, metrics, blockers, decisions needed, next steps.
- Bias toward clarity and no-spin reporting.
- Must include: decision-needed page or explicit next owner.

## Training Deck

- Decision: help the audience understand and retain a process.
- Sequence: objective, context, core concepts, examples, exercise, recap, action.
- Bias toward teaching flow and repetition of key concepts.
- Must include: audience fit, examples, recap, and what to do next after the deck.

## Keynote or Demo Talk

- Decision: create belief, excitement, or memorable understanding.
- Sequence: hook, tension, reframing, reveal, proof, payoff, close.
- Bias toward rhythm, visuals, and memorable transitions.
- Must include: cover, at least one high-impact visual reveal, proof, and a close that lands without live narration.
