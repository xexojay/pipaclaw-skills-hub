# Typography and Font Safety

Typography is not just aesthetics. It directly affects readability, polish, and export compatibility.

## Default Rule

- Choose fonts based on language and delivery surface first.
- Choose mood second.
- Do not let a fashionable desktop font break mobile or WeChat readability.

## Chinese Decks

Prefer conservative editable font choices for body text and dense labels.

Safe bias for Chinese editable decks:
- titles: `思源黑体`, `微软雅黑`, `苹方`
- body: same-family sans serif is preferred over mixed novelty fonts

Avoid:
- mixing too many Chinese font families in one deck
- using an English-first default font as the main Chinese body font
- relying on a font that is unlikely to exist in mobile viewers when editable text must survive

## English Decks

- Use one display family plus one text family at most.
- Avoid defaulting to generic modern Office fonts if the deck is trying to feel branded or premium.

## Mixed Chinese and English

- Make sure Latin text and Chinese text feel like one system.
- Avoid obvious clashes where English headings feel modern but Chinese body text falls back awkwardly.

## WeChat-Safe Bias

- Prefer stability over personality for editable text.
- If decorative typography is important, keep it to non-critical headings or rasterized decorative layers.
- Never make the page dependent on a fragile font to look finished.

## Review Questions

- If the target viewer lacks the preferred font, does the page still look intentional?
- Are title and body fonts consistent with the deck's scene?
- Does the typography help scanning, or does it just look "different"?
