# PPT First Principles and Quality Bar

PPT is not decoration. It is a decision interface that helps an audience understand, trust, and act.

## First Principles

- `Decision-first`: every deck must point to a concrete decision or action.
- `Audience-fit`: wording, depth, and visuals must match who decides and who executes.
- `Proof over claims`: key statements need evidence, assumptions, or explicit uncertainty.
- `One slide, one job`: each page should do one thing only.
- `Signal over noise`: remove content that does not move the argument.

## Quality Bar (Must Pass)

- `Clear ask`: final action and owner are explicit.
- `Narrative flow`: context -> problem -> options -> recommendation -> next step.
- `Evidence integrity`: important numbers have source paths or assumption labels.
- `Visual usability`: readable typography, no stretched images, no random style mixing.
- `Operational readiness`: deck can be presented without filling blanks live.

## Failure Patterns (Must Reject)

- Beautiful slides without a decision.
- Data pages with no source or metric definition.
- Mixed audience levels in one storyline (exec + beginner details without layering).
- Placeholder text, missing sections, or off-topic generated images.
- Decorative charts that do not answer a question.

## Acceptance Question

Before export, ask internally:
"If this deck is shown now, will the target audience make the intended decision with confidence?"
If not, continue iteration.
