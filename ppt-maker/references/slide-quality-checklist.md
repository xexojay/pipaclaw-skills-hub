# Slide Quality Checklist

- Give each slide one job.
- Make the title say the conclusion, not the topic.
- Before layout, define the page's `conclusion`, `support`, `visual role`, and `page recipe`.
- Prefer evidence, examples, or visuals over dense paragraphs.
- Do not let key slides rely on bullets alone when a screenshot, table, process map, case card, or diagram would make the point clearer.
- Remove bullets that only restate the title.
- Use charts only when they sharpen the argument.
- Do not repeat the same page grammar too many times in a row.
- Make demo, case, and workflow pages understandable even if the audience reads them without the presenter.
- End with a clear ask, next step, or takeaway.
- Check whether the presenter can explain the slide in one breath.
- Keep footer and chrome low-noise; repeated labels should not compete with the message.
- Cut any slide that does not move the audience toward the target decision.
- If WeChat is part of delivery, check whether the slide would still feel visually intentional if text rendering weakens.
- Do not make critical pages look empty when stripped down to their editable essentials.
